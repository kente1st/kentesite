vehicle financing

Vehicle financing is provided through the following ways:

  • Dealership Financing: When you visit a Toyota dealership to purchase a vehicle, the dealership's finance and insurance (F&I) department will work with you to explore financing options. The dealership will gather information about your financial situation, credit history, and desired terms to determine the financing options available to you. The dealership will submit your application to Toyota Financial Services on your behalf.
  • Loan and Lease Options: Toyota Financial Services offers both traditional auto loans and lease options to suit different customer preferences. Auto Loans: If you choose to finance your vehicle through an auto loan, TFS will provide the funds to the dealership, and you'll make monthly payments to TFS over the agreed-upon term. Leasing: If you prefer to lease a Toyota vehicle, TFS offers lease programs where you make monthly payments for the use of the vehicle over a specific term. Leasing often provides lower monthly payments compared to purchasing.
  • Credit Approval and Terms: TFS will evaluate your creditworthiness based on factors such as credit score, income, and debt-to-income ratio. If approved, TFS will determine the financing terms, including interest rate, down payment amount, loan/lease duration, and any additional fees. The terms offered may vary based on your creditworthiness and other factors, such as the vehicle model, market conditions, and promotions.
  • Online Account Management: Once you secure financing through Toyota Financial Services, you can create an online account to manage your loan or lease. The online portal allows you to make payments, view your payment history, check your remaining balance, and access other account-related information.
  • Special Programs and Incentives: TFS occasionally offers special financing programs and incentives, such as low-interest rates, cash-back offers, and deferred payment options. These programs can vary based on location, vehicle models, and promotional periods.

vehicle maintenance and services

Here are some key aspects of vehicle maintenance and services:

  • Regular Maintenance Schedule: Follow the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule outlined in your vehicle's owner's manual. The schedule typically includes periodic inspections, oil and filter changes, tire rotations, and other routine maintenance tasks. Regular maintenance helps identify potential issues early on and keeps your vehicle running smoothly.

  • Fluid Checks and Replacements: Regularly check and top up fluid levels such as coolant, brake fluid, power steering fluid, and windshield washer fluid. Replace fluids as needed to maintain proper levels and ensure optimal performance.
  • Tire Maintenance: Check tire pressure regularly and adjust it according to the manufacturer's recommendations. Rotate tires at regular intervals to ensure even wear. Inspect tires for tread depth, signs of damage, and consider replacing them when necessary.
  • Brakes and Brake System: Regularly inspect the brake system, including brake pads, rotors, and brake fluid. Replace brake pads when they are worn, and have the brake system checked if you notice any unusual noises or reduced braking performance.
  • Battery Maintenance: Check the battery regularly for signs of corrosion, damage, or weak performance. Clean battery terminals if necessary and ensure a secure connection. Replace the battery if it is nearing the end of its lifespan or if it fails to hold a charge.
  • Air Filter Replacement: Replace the engine air filter and cabin air filter as recommended by the manufacturer. A clean air filter improves engine performance and cabin air quality.
  • Wheel Alignment and Suspension: Periodically check and adjust wheel alignment to ensure even tire wear and proper vehicle handling. Inspect the suspension system for signs of wear, such as worn-out bushings or shocks, and address any issues promptly.
  • Regular Inspections: Conduct regular visual inspections of your vehicle, including lights, wipers, belts, hoses, and other components. Look for signs of leaks, unusual noises, or warning lights on the dashboard.
  • Professional Servicing: Consider taking your vehicle to a certified mechanic or authorized service center for regular maintenance and inspections. Professionals have the expertise and equipment to perform thorough checks, identify potential problems, and provide accurate repairs or replacements.
